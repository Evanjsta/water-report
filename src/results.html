The Shearwater Pool Report

Chlorine in the water is the most important measurement for swimmer safety. Chlorine kills viruses and bacteria, the things that make you sick.

The results from the chlorine test should be between 2.0-4.0. The closer to 4.0 the better. Slightly higher than 4.0 isnâ€™t a bad thing but it just means more chlorine than is needed.

We are testing for free chlorine because that is what we care about as swimmers.

The swimmer also cares about the PH level. Too high or two low a PH will be a skin and eye irritant and can special problems for female swimmers.

Last is calcium hardness level. We as swimmers care about this because if this is too low than skin will start to feel dry and itchy. We want to be above 225.
